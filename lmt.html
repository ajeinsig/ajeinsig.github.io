Large Millimeter Telescope

Located on Volcán Sierra Negra in Puebla, Mexico, the Large Millimeter Telescope Alfonso Serrano is an impressive 50m in diameter and sensitive to light at ~1-4mm. It is a binational collaboration between the University of Massachusetts, Amherst (UMass) and the Instituto Nacional de Astrofísica, Óptica y Electrónica (INAOE). Each oberving season, the LMT spends hundreds of hours observing clouds of molecular gas, distant dusty galaxies, and supermassive black holes. Nearly all of the data that this telescope collects is looked over by myself and my team before being passed on to the PIs of the respective projects. I also assist in updating and improving calibration procedures, and monitoring instrument performance.

Through my work at the LMT, I have learned more about radio astornomy instrumentation, calibration, and observation than possible from a lecture hall. If you're interested in hearing more, be sure to stop by our booth at AAS 245 in National Harbor, MD.
