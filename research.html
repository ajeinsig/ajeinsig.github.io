Research

Emission-Line Diagnostics

AGN and star-forming galaxies usually separate themselves out on a traditional BPT diagram due to the different mechanisms powering their emission. However, there are some objects that are ambiguous in nature, located between the Kauffmann (Kauffmann et. al., 2001) and Kewley (Kewley et. al. 2003) lines on the BPT diagram. These lines represent an empirical separation of AGN and star-forming galaxies, and the maximum possible starburst emission a galaxy can have, respectively. Galaxies located between these are widely considered to be composite objects; objects that contain both AGN and star-formation activity and may represent an evolutionary stage in the assumed SMBH-host galaxy connection.

My work focuses on these objects and, using different emission features in their spectra, attempts to classify the sources of their emission.
